Uber in New York - A Moving City
    By Jordan Esh

Uber is the world's leading ridesharing service, providing a cheaper and more user-friendly alternative to taxis. Every day, all around the world thousands of people use the service for their daily commutes. We picked New York City, one of the world's busiest cities, and well known for its taxis usage, as our case study, so with that in mind, let's look at how people are starting to use Uber.

New York City is located in the North Eastern part of the United States of America, in the state of New York. It has a population of over 8.5 Million people, is the most densely populated major city in the US, and is split into five different metropolitan areas, known as Boroughs.

The five Boroughs of New York are:

The Bronx
Brooklyn
Manhattan
Staten Island
Queens

Manhattan is the busiest, being the city's main financial hub, and Staten Island is the least, with a larger proportion of residential areas. Each of these Boroughs is further made up of different neighborhoods.

The flow of traffic fluctuates throughout the day, with large spikes in all Boroughs around the morning and afternoon peaks, around 9 AM and 5 PM.

Queens and Brooklyn maintain a relatively high number of trips for longer into the evening, as people travel in and around to Entertainment areas.

John F. Kennedy International Airport has no residents, so the values for it in the above charts are simply the unnormalised number of trips at a given time.
